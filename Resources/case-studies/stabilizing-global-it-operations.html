Stabilizing Global IT Operations for a Global Professional Certification Body

160+ Countries Served
24x7 Managed Services

Overview

A globally recognized professional certification body, operating across 160+ countries and serving millions of members, was facing critical challenges with its IT infrastructure. Fragmented regional IT systems and inconsistent service delivery had led to recurring outages in exam delivery platforms, member portals, and certification workflows — directly impacting the organization's credibility and member trust.

The organization partnered with Parkar to stabilize and unify its global IT operations, implementing 24x7 managed services and standardized delivery processes that could scale across diverse regulatory environments and time zones.

Challenge

Years of rapid global expansion had outpaced the organization's IT capabilities, creating a complex web of regional systems and processes that were difficult to manage, monitor, and secure. Core challenges included:

  • Regional Silos: Each region operated its own IT infrastructure, tools, and support processes, leading to inconsistent service levels, duplicated effort, and a lack of visibility into global system health.
  • Service Breakdowns: Critical platforms — including online exam delivery, member registration portals, and certification issuance systems — experienced frequent outages and performance degradation, eroding member confidence and creating reputational risk.
  • Compliance Risks: Operating across 160+ countries required adherence to a patchwork of data privacy, security, and regulatory standards. Fragmented IT made it difficult to enforce consistent compliance policies and respond to audit requirements.
  • Global Complexity: Supporting millions of members across every time zone demanded round-the-clock availability, multilingual support capabilities, and the ability to coordinate incident response across geographically distributed teams.

Solution

Parkar designed and implemented a comprehensive IT operations transformation, replacing fragmented regional approaches with a unified, globally managed service model.

Unified IT Operations

A centralized IT operations framework was established to consolidate monitoring, incident management, and change control across all regions. A single-pane-of-glass dashboard provided real-time visibility into system health, performance metrics, and SLA adherence worldwide, enabling rapid identification and resolution of issues before they impacted members.

24x7 Managed Services

Parkar deployed a follow-the-sun managed services model, ensuring continuous coverage across all time zones. Dedicated teams provided proactive monitoring, L1 through L3 support, and escalation management around the clock — dramatically reducing mean time to detect (MTTD) and mean time to resolve (MTTR) for critical incidents.

Standardized Global Delivery

ITIL-aligned processes were implemented across all regions, standardizing incident, problem, change, and release management. Automation was introduced for routine tasks such as patching, backups, and health checks, freeing up skilled resources to focus on strategic initiatives and continuous improvement.

Digital Services Enablement

Beyond stabilization, Parkar helped modernize key member-facing platforms, improving the reliability and performance of exam delivery systems, certification portals, and self-service tools. This included cloud migration support, performance tuning, and the introduction of resilient architectures to handle peak demand during global exam windows.

Key Results

  • Stabilized Operations Across 160+ Countries: Unified monitoring and managed services eliminated recurring outages, delivering consistent, reliable service to members worldwide.
  • Improved Member Access and Experience: Exam delivery platforms and member portals achieved significantly higher uptime and faster response times, restoring member confidence and reducing support ticket volumes.
  • Reduced Compliance Risk: Standardized processes and centralized governance made it easier to enforce data privacy and security policies, streamline audit responses, and maintain regulatory compliance across jurisdictions.
  • Enhanced Organizational Trust: Reliable, always-on IT services reinforced the organization's reputation as a trusted global certification authority, strengthening relationships with members, employers, and regulatory bodies.
  • Increased Operational Efficiency: Automation and process standardization reduced manual effort, lowered operational costs, and improved the speed and quality of IT service delivery across the enterprise.

The Future

  • Personalized Member Portals: Leveraging data and AI to deliver tailored learning paths, certification recommendations, and career development resources to each member based on their professional profile and goals.
  • AI-Driven Assessment Innovation: Exploring AI-powered adaptive testing and automated exam proctoring to enhance the integrity, accessibility, and scalability of global certification programs.
  • Advanced Analytics and Insights: Building a unified analytics platform to provide leadership with real-time visibility into member engagement, certification trends, and operational performance across all regions.
  • IT as a Strategic Enabler: Evolving IT from a cost center to a strategic partner, enabling the organization to launch new digital products, expand into new markets, and respond faster to evolving member needs.
