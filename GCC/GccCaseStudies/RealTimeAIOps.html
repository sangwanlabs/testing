Overview

This case study illustrates how a Fortune 500 manufacturer achieved enterprise-grade observability and automated incident response through a fully managed AIOps GCC — enabling real-time insights and operational resilience at scale.

Challenge

Overwhelmed by 50,000+ monthly alerts and siloed observability platforms across plants. The existing setup lacked correlation between IT and OT signals, making root cause analysis slow and manual — leading to cascading failures and unplanned downtime.

Solution

Parkar implemented an AIOps GCC delivering:

  • Unified IT/OT observability using standardized telemetry
  • Automated alert triage and fault resolution
  • Cross-functional teams including platform engineering and SRE

Key Results

  • 60% reduction in manual escalations
  • 3x improvement in incident response time
  • Enterprise-grade compliance and real-time insights

How We Did It

Unified Observability, Automated Response

Parkar stood up a dedicated AIOps GCC team combining platform engineers, SREs, and data engineers to build a unified observability layer that bridged IT and operational technology signals.

The system automatically triages, correlates, and resolves alerts — freeing engineering teams to focus on strategic improvements rather than fire-fighting.

Unified IT/OT Observability

Standardized telemetry pipelines ingesting signals from both IT infrastructure and operational technology across plants.

Automated Alert Triage

ML-powered correlation engine that triages 50K+ monthly alerts, reducing noise and surfacing actionable incidents.

Cross-functional SRE Teams

Platform engineering and site reliability engineering teams embedded within the GCC for continuous improvement.
